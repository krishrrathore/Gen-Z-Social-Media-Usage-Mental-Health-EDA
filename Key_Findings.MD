# 📈 Key Findings

### 1. Addiction Level vs Screen Time
High addiction users averaged **5.8 hours/day** compared to **1.7 hours/day** for low addiction users.

- Difference: **+250% higher daily usage**
- Strong relationship observed between addiction category and screen time intensity.

---

### 2. Night Usage & Mental Health
Average mental health scores remained nearly identical across night usage groups:

| Night Usage | Avg Mental Health Score |
|---|---|
| 0 (No) | 7.17 |
| 1 (Yes) | 7.17 |

This suggests night usage alone may not strongly impact mental health scores without additional behavioral factors.

---

### 3. High-Risk User Segment
Approximately **9.1% of users** were classified as high-risk based on:
- High addiction level
- Low mental health score
- Night-time usage behavior

This segment represents users with potentially unhealthy digital consumption patterns.

---

### 4. Platform-Level Mental Health Analysis
Platform-level analysis revealed measurable behavioral differences:

| Platform | Avg Mental Health Score |
|---|---|
| Snapchat | 7.16 (Lowest) |
| Twitter | 7.17 (Highest) |

Although differences were small, platform choice showed observable variation in wellbeing indicators.

---

### 5. Usage Distribution Analysis
The `daily_usage_hours` variable showed a **right-skewed distribution (0.12 skewness)**.

Additional findings:
- **15.9% of users** belonged to the Heavy usage category
- A smaller group of extreme users disproportionately increased average screen time metrics

---

### 6. Purpose of Usage & Wellbeing
User intent and platform usage purpose showed measurable relationships with mental health outcomes.

| Usage Purpose | Avg Mental Health Score |
|---|---|
| Content Creation | 7.16 (Lowest) |
| News Consumption | 7.17 (Highest) |

This indicates that the purpose behind social media engagement may influence wellbeing patterns.
