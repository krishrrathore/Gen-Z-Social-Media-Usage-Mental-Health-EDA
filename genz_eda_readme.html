key findings
01 — Addiction & Usage
High addiction users spend significantly more hours/day on social media than low addiction users — the gap is consistent and measurable.
02 — Night Usage
Night scrolling is linked to lower mental health scores. Users who use social media before sleep consistently score below average.
03 — High-Risk Segment
A meaningful % of users meet all three risk conditions simultaneously: high addiction + below-median mental health + night usage.
04 — Platform Matters
There's a measurable gap in mental health scores across platforms — your platform of choice is not neutral.
05 — Skewed Distribution
Usage hours are right-skewed — a small group of extreme users pulls the average up. Most users are Light to Moderate.
06 — Purpose & Wellbeing
Why you use social media matters. Different purposes (entertainment vs connection) correlate with noticeably different mental health outcomes.
